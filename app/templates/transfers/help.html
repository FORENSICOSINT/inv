Conversion Factor Help

Understanding how QTY × CF = Base Units works
What is a Conversion Factor (CF)?
Core Formula: QTY × CF = Base Units

The system converts all quantities to base units before processing.

Why Conversion Factors Exist:

Stores often sell, store, or transfer the same product in different units:

  • Base unit: Piece (PC), Kilogram, Bottle
  • Larger unit: Box, Pack, Carton
  • Even larger: Pallet, Case

But inventory accuracy requires that the system tracks stock in ONE base unit.

Simple Example: Sugar Sachets
Unit Conversion Factor (CF) Meaning
Sachet 1 Base unit (1 sachet = 1 base unit)
Pack 10 1 Pack = 10 sachets
Box 100 1 Box = 100 sachets
How QTY × CF Works:
Scenario 1: Transfer 3 boxes of sugar
  • QTY = 3 boxes
  • CF = 100 (1 box = 100 sachets)
  • Calculation: 3 × 100 = 300 sachets
  • The system deducts 300 sachets from Store A
  • The system adds 300 sachets to Store B
Real-World Analogy

Think of money: Base unit = cents.
You store $3.50 as 350 cents in the database, not as "3 notes + 5 coins". Same principle applies to inventory.
